Delaware Malayalee Association (Delma), is a cultural non profit organization of people hailing from great state of Kerala also known as God's Own Country.Delma is based out of Delaware and bridges families of Kerala origin spread all over Delaware and surrounding areas of Maryland, New Jersey and Pennsylvania. We organize numerous cultural activities all around the year. And make it possible for the continued expression of the Malayalee heritage to one and all, especially to the younger generation. Kerala claims to have a culture the history of which runs into the dim recesses of antiquity. Delma promotes and propagates Kerala culture by conducting pristine cultural programs and organizing get-together and picnics. . The mission of Delma is to serve as a forum to facilitate and enable social and cultural interactions among the member families and to motivate the kids to learn Malayalam language and Kerala traditions.

Delma Office Bearers

Delaware Malayalee Association (DELMA) is a registered non-profit, non-religious organization that aims to unite malayalees in Delware and educate the next generation of the rich cultural heritage of Kerala. Delma also seeks to foster good relations within our diverse American community through mutual friendship and cultural exchange. Delma is not owned by an individual or run by a handful of people. Every year before the second week of May Delma conducts a general body meeting. In this meeting all the Members of Delma are invited. And these members select the office bearers (also known as committee) to run the organization for the new financial year. Delma has a set of by laws or guidelines and strictly follows it to elect these committee members and to be followed in functioning of Delma.

Activities Overview

Delaware Malayalees are an active bunch of people. And as you see below there are a lot of activities for you to join. To join or for more information on any of the following events send mail to info@delma.org

  • Dance & Arts

    Delma possesses a lot of talented musicians and dancers who participate in cultural programs in and around Delaware. 'DHWANI' is a music troupe formed by members of Delma.

    NRITHYA Nrithya dance group was founded in Delaware in 2012. Formed by a group of like minded people with utmost passion in dancing. Although they are more inclined towards classical and semi classical numbers, some of their performances include energy packed Bollywood numbers. If you are interested in any such events feel free to contact them.

  • Football

    New Castle United (NCU) soccer team is predominantly malayalees but anyone with interest in soccer is welcome to join. NCU team participate in soccer tournaments in Philadelphia,Newyork and Baltimore.
    Schedule : Summer 2-3 days a week Winter : Weekends.

  • Volleyball

    Volleyball team plays twice a week in summer. We have active male and female participants for Volleyball friendlies. Our teams participate in local delaware tournaments as well as malayalee volleyball tournaments in Philadelphia, New Jersey and IAAD events.

  • Cricket

    Delma Members are active participants of Delaware United Cricket League (Tennis Ball) and Philadelphia Cricket League (Cricket Ball). If you are interested please drop us a mail at info@delma.org

  • Cycling

    Our cycling team is dedicated to the positive experiences of cycling. Join our cycling team if you own a street bike(cycle). We also organize group cycling fun events in and around Newark & Newcastle areas.

  • Cheettukali

    Friendly card games are played whenever there is an oppportunity . Rummy , 28 and 56 are the favorites among participants. If you are interested to join or organize please drop us a mail at info@delma.org

Delaware Malayalee Association (Delma), is proud to present 'Malayalam Paathashaala' (മലയാളം പാഠശാല) for the kids of Delaware. On February 22, 2014 Malayalam class was inaugrated by President Mohan Shenoi at the Hockessin Hindu Temple in Delaware. Prominent members from Malayalee community of Delaware were present at the auspicious event. The Malayalam classes was started off with 'Aksharamaala' (അക്ഷരമാല) by DELMA's own dear faculty Reshmi Prathib. The program is an ongoing success thanks to the dedication and honest effort from Ajitha Menon, Julie Wilson, Abhitha Jose, Biju Das, Sreejith Sreekumaran & Niveda Rajan to name a few. DELMA is specially thankful to all parents who accepted this idea whole heartedly and encouraged their kids to learn Malayalam.
